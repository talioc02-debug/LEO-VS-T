Article 1 · On war & peace

Pope and President: dueling messages on war

News Desk · Sunday Focus report

During a high-profile international trip, a news programme drew attention to the contrast between two of the world’s most-watched voices. The segment, titled “Pope & President’s dueling messages on war,” reported that the visit had been overshadowed by the war dominating the headlines.

According to the report, Pope Leo XIV — the first American-born pope, elected in 2025 — has repeatedly called for a ceasefire, for dialogue between enemies, and for the protection of ordinary civilians. He has framed peace not as politics but as a moral duty, and has urged the powerful to choose negotiation over force.

“The Pope and the President are not reading from the same script.”

President Donald Trump, the report noted, has stressed strength and deterrence, arguing that lasting peace comes from negotiating from a position of power and that pressure — not appeals — is what ends conflicts. Commentators said the two leaders were drawing a sharp contrast in tone, even when both said they wanted the fighting to stop.

The broadcast described the result as dueling messages: one voice raising concerns about the human cost of the war, the other setting the tone for a tougher approach. With cameras following every word, the journalists asked which message would ultimately shape the conversation about the conflict.

Article 2 · On technology & people

Italy ties its AI rulebook to the Pope’s human-centred vision

Decode39 · Technology & policy

Italy has become the first country in the European Union to pass a comprehensive national law on artificial intelligence. According to Decode39, the government has openly tied the new rulebook to Pope Leo XIV’s “human-centred” vision of technology.

The central idea is simple: AI must serve people, not replace human judgement. The law highlights human oversight, the protection of workers and children, and respect for fundamental rights. Supporters say it is designed to safeguard human dignity as machines become more powerful.

“Technology should put the person at the centre — not the algorithm.”

The connection to the Pope is deliberate. By taking the name Leo, the pope echoed Leo XIII, who addressed the upheavals of the industrial revolution more than a century ago. Pope Leo XIV has warned that artificial intelligence is the defining challenge to human dignity of our time, and has urged leaders to make sure innovation does not leave people behind.

Italian officials argued that finding common ground between fast-moving technology and unchanging human values is exactly the point of the new rules. The result, supporters say, is a human-centred rulebook for a machine age.

📰 Source note: Paraphrased from the Decode39 article “Italy ties AI rulebook to Pope Leo XIV’s human-centered vision” and related public statements. Pair it with the second video as a listening warm-up.

The link between the two stories

In both stories the Pope makes the same argument in a different setting. Faced with the violence of war and the disruption of technology, he keeps returning to one word: human. Whether the danger is a missile or an algorithm, his message is that people — their dignity, their safety, their future — must stay at the centre.

Language focus · Verbs to report the news

How journalists report what people say

Reporters rarely write “he said.” They choose a verb that tells you how something was said. Each one carries a different attitude.

Reporting verbIt means…ItalianExample from the news
to call forto publicly demand / requestchiedere, invocareThe Pope called for an immediate ceasefire.
to urgeto strongly advise / push someone to actesortare, sollecitareHe urged leaders not to leave people behind.
to stressto give special importance to a pointsottolineareTrump stressed strength and deterrence.
to warnto tell someone about a dangeravvertire, mettere in guardiaThe Pope warned that AI threatens human dignity.
to highlightto draw attention to somethingevidenziareThe law highlights human oversight.
to argueto give reasons for a positionsostenere, argomentareOfficials argued that values must guide technology.
to raise concernsto express worry publiclysollevare preoccupazioniThe report raised concerns about civilians.

After you read · Speaking & writing

Make the connection

  1. Comprehension: In your own words, what are the “dueling messages” in the first story?
  2. Comprehension: Why did Italy connect an AI law to a religious vision?
  3. Link the stories: The Pope speaks about war and about AI. What single idea connects the two? Find the word that appears in both articles.
  4. Report it: Imagine you are a journalist. Write three sentences about today’s news using three different reporting verbs (e.g. urge, warn, stress, call for, highlight, argue).
  5. Opinion: Do you agree that technology should be “human-centred”? Should the same idea guide decisions about war? Why / why not?
